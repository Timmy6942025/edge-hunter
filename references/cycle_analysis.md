# Cycle Analysis

## Purpose

Markets move in cycles. Understanding where you are in a cycle — and when cycles are about to turn — is one of the highest-value analytical skills. Cycles give you a probability-weighted view of forward returns and help you avoid the most common mistake: buying at cycle tops or selling at cycle bottoms.

This file feeds **Step 1 (Orient)** of the reasoning loop — before you build a thesis, you need to understand the cycle context.

---

## Cycle Types Across Asset Classes

### Equities

#### Earnings Revision Cycle

Analysts revise earnings estimates over a 6-12 month cycle. The key signal: are estimates going up or down?

| Signal | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|
| 12-month forward estimates rising | Bull — earnings momentum supports stock |
| Estimates rising but stock flat | Market is discounting the improvement |
| Estimates peaking before stock peaks | Leading indicator — stock follows estimates |
| Estimates falling | Bear — earnings recession typically 6-18 months |

#### Bull/Bear Market Cycle

| Phase | Duration | Characteristics | Equity Behavior |
|-------|----------|----------------|----------------|
| Early recovery | 3-12 months | Low valuations, fear, credit expansion | Strongest returns |
| Mid-cycle expansion | 12-24 months | Earnings growth, rising margins | Good returns, selective |
| Late cycle | 6-18 months | Peak margins, capacity constraints | Volatile, defensives lead |
| Bear market | 6-24 months | Earnings decline, credit tightening | Negative returns |
| Accumulation | 6-18 months | Valuations trough, fear persists | Low returns, high variance |

**Duration note**: The average bull is ~4-5 years, bears ~1.5 years. But cycles compress or extend based on policy. Don't use duration to time — use valuation and credit conditions.

#### Sector Rotation Cycle

Sectors rotate predictably through the economic cycle:

1. **Early cycle** (6-12mo after trough): Financials, Industrials, Discretionary
2. **Mid cycle** (12-24mo): Technology, Comm Services, Materials
3. **Late cycle** (6-12mo before peak): Healthcare, Utilities, Staples
4. **Recession**: No sector does well; bonds outperform

**Signal**: When defensives start outperforming growth sectors, the cycle is late. When the reverse happens, the cycle is early.

### Bonds / Rates

#### Rate Cycle

Central banks raise rates to fight inflation, lower rates to stimulate growth. The rate cycle drives all other asset classes.

**Key indicators**: Fed Funds Rate vs. neutral rate, 2Y vs. 10Y curve shape, inflation breakevens, PMI.

**Cycle phases**:
1. **Rate rise**: Duration is enemy; short-duration outperforms
2. **Peak rate**: Long-duration becomes attractive
3. **Rate decline**: Duration is friend; long-duration outperforms
4. **Zero/negative rate**: Search for yield; credit spreads compress

#### Credit Cycle

Credit spreads widen in recessions and compress in expansions.

**Leading indicators**:
- HY spread > 500bps: stress building
- IG spread > 150bps: meaningful tightening
- CCC/BB default rate rising: cycle turning
- Loan officer survey (Fed SLOOS): banks tightening → recession signal

### Commodities

#### Super-Cycle (20-30 year)

| Super-Cycle | Driver | Peak |
|-------------|--------|------|
| 1970s | Arab oil embargo, Stagflation | 1980 |
| 2000s | China industrialization | 2008 |
| 2020s | Energy transition capex | TBD |

**How to identify**: Sustained multi-year demand surge that requires multi-year supply response.

#### Inventory Cycle (3-5 year)

Commodities move: deficit (prices rise) → build → surplus (prices fall) → deficit.

**The signal**: When inventory is at multi-year lows and demand is rising = early deficit phase (bullish). When inventory is at multi-year highs and demand is slowing = surplus phase (bearish).

#### Seasonal Patterns

| Commodity | Seasonal Pattern |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Natural gas | Demand peaks Jan/Feb, lows Apr/May |
| Gasoline | Demand peaks May-Sept |
| Agriculture | Planting Apr-Jun = bottom; harvest Sept-Nov = top |
| Metals | Infrastructure spending = Q1-Q2 strength |

### Crypto

#### Halving Cycle (4 year)

| Phase | Timing | Historical Behavior |
|-------|--------|-------------------|
| Pre-halving accumulation | 12-18 months before | Sideways or rising |
| Post-halving run | 12-18 months after | Explosive bull run |
| Cycle peak | 12-18 months after halving | Parabolic, unsustainable |
| Bear market | 12-18 months | 70-85% drawdown |
| Bottom and recovery | 12-18 months | Sideways accumulation |

**The caveat**: The halving cycle was disrupted in 2025-2026 when BTC peaked before the expected post-halving acceleration and crashed 50%. Institutionalization via ETFs changed the cycle structure — it's less purely supply-driven now.

**What changed**: ETFs introduced large, regular institutional flows that can overwhelm the halving supply shock. M2 liquidity correlation is now stronger than the halving signal.

#### Adoption S-Curve

- **Early phase** (< 5%): Price driven by believers; volatile; fundamentals don't matter much
- **Inflection** (5-25%): Rapid adoption; reflexivity active; price outruns fundamentals
- **Maturation** (25-50%): Adoption still growing but decelerating; price more correlated with traditional assets
- **Saturation** (> 50%): Growth slows to GDP-like; price follows macro conditions

## Second-Order Cycle Interactions

### Yield Curve → Earnings → Equities

1. Yield curve inverts (2Y > 10Y)
2. Banks' NIM compresses → financial sector underperforms
3. Tight credit → earnings growth slows
4. Earnings growth slows → equities derate
5. 12-18 months after inversion: recession → earnings decline → bear market

### M2 Liquidity → Risk Assets → Global Growth

1. Global M2 expands (central bank easing, fiscal stimulus)
2. Liquidity flows to risk assets
3. Risk asset wealth effect → consumer spending
4. Consumer spending → global growth
5. Growth → central banks tighten → M2 contracts
6. Liquidity withdrawal → risk assets fall

**The signal**: Global M2 growth turning negative is the most reliable precursor of crypto and high-beta equity drawdowns.

### Earnings Revision → Capex → GDP

1. Earnings revisions start rising
2. Management teams increase capex guidance
3. Capex flows to industrials, suppliers, employment
4. Employment + wages → consumer spending
5. Consumer spending → more earnings growth

## When Cycles Break

### What Causes Cycle Acceleration

- **Unexpected policy stimulus**: Aggressive rate cuts or fiscal stimulus can extend a bull cycle by years
- **Technology disruption**: A new technology creates an investment super-cycle (AI infrastructure 2023-2026)
- **Geopolitical shock**: Supply chain disruptions create commodity super-cycles

### What Causes Cycle Compression

- **Policy error**: Central bank overtightening causes rapid bear markets (2008, 2022)
- **Credit crisis**: Unexpected default or contagion accelerates the credit cycle
- **Technology substitution**: A new entrant with lower cost structure disrupts incumbents

### The Sahm Rule Recession Indicator

When the 3-month average unemployment rate rises 0.5pp above its 12-month low, a recession has begun. No false positives since the 1970s.

## Practical Cycle Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Dominant Cycle

- Equities: earnings revision cycle + interest rate cycle
- Bonds: rate cycle + credit cycle
- Commodities: inventory cycle + super-cycle
- Crypto: halving cycle + liquidity cycle + adoption S-curve

### Step 2: Determine Where in the Cycle You Are

| Indicator | Early Cycle | Mid Cycle | Late Cycle |
|-----------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| Valuations | Cheap | Fair | Expensive |
| Earnings revisions | Rising | Peak | Falling |
| Credit conditions | Easing | Neutral | Tightening |
| Sector leadership | Cyclical | Growth | Defensive |

### Step 3: Identify the Most Probable Next Transition

- Valuation extremes + credit tightening = cycle turn likely
- Valuations cheap + policy easing = cycle continuation likely
- Mixed signals = pass or bet small

### Step 4: Weight Your Thesis by Cycle Probability

- Strong thesis + cycle favors you = larger position
- Strong thesis + cycle against you = smaller position or pass
- Weak thesis + cycle favors you = consider if thesis is truly weak
- Weak thesis + cycle against you = pass

## Common Cycle Analysis Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Cyclicality Confusion
Treating a secular trend as cyclical (or vice versa). AI infrastructure looks like a cycle — but if AI genuinely transforms the economy, it could be a 10-year secular trend. Don't exit a secular trend on cycle concerns.

### Mistake 2: Cycle Duration Anchoring
Assuming a cycle will last exactly as long as historical averages. Use cycle phase, not duration, to time.

### Mistake 3: Mean Reversion False Signals
Buying because something is oversold without asking why it's oversold. In a bear market, oversold can stay oversold longer than you think.

### Mistake 4: Cycle Stacking
Combining cycle signals pointing in different directions and hoping they net out. If equity cycle says buy but credit cycle says sell, you have confusion — not a signal.

### Mistake 5: Ignoring Policy Feedback Loops
Central banks and governments actively respond to cycles. Policy is endogenous to the cycle, not exogenous.

## Integration with the Reasoning Loop

This file feeds **Step 1 (Orient)**. Before building a thesis, answer:

1. What type of cycle is this asset in?
2. Where in the cycle am I?
3. What's the probability of a cycle transition in my investment horizon?
4. How does the cycle context change my thesis confidence?

A strong thesis in a hostile cycle context should be sized smaller or rejected. A moderate thesis in a favorable cycle context can be sized more aggressively.
